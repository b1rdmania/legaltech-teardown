## Bottom line (read this)
The market is splitting cleanly into **un-governed infrastructure** and **governed apps** — and nobody owns the **governed *layer*** in between (audit, privilege posture, accountability, supervised autonomy, on the rails). The 80/20 flip (law going 80% AI / 20% human) turns that layer from nice-to-have into mandatory: when AI does the bulk of the work, regulators and insurers will *require* the guardrails. **Highest-conviction move: build that governed layer.** Use one timely acquisition (Moonlit, raising now) for the data rail and one partnership (DeepJudge) for retrieval, so you're building on picks-and-shovels rather than reinventing them.

## White space (build the lab around this)
- **The governed layer — "what a regulator or an insurer needs to see."** Audit trail, privilege posture, supervised-autonomy controls, regulator/insurer-ready evidence. Everyone is building either un-governed infra or governed *apps*; no one owns the governed *layer* the apps and agents sit on. Stella is positioned to take it because the supervised-autonomy thesis *is* the lab's posture, and the infra plays above (Moonlit's data rail, DeepJudge's retrieval) are buildable-on, not competitive with it.
- **The demand engine is structural, not speculative.** The 80/20 flip means the guardrails get productised whether Stella builds them or not — the only question is who owns the layer when regulators/insurers start requiring it.

## The other model worth watching (not a Stella move)
- **Moritz** — the "be the regulated AI firm" play (Thesis 2). It's the *inverse* of the lab's posture: Moritz *becomes* the AI firm; Stella *arms* the firms and owns the layer beneath them. Useful as a strategic mirror (build-the-layer vs. be-the-firm), not as an acquisition target.

## Avoid / no-regret passes
- **The dead segment** — standalone vertical LLM apps with no interop (Crimson, Emma, PhaseLaw, Pivot, Mage). Features, not companies; a frontier-model release from gone. Build, don't buy.
- **Clonable horizontals** — "spin up bespoke legal agents" (Airia, Eudia, Newcode, Wexler, Casey). The most copyable shape there is; a quarter from commoditised.
- **Diagramming-as-data** (Structureflow, Jigsaw) — good businesses, capped ceilings, closed surfaces. Not where the thesis points.
- **Deliberately out of scope:** the $500M+ Series B incumbents. A take on the giants helps no one and just picks a fight.
